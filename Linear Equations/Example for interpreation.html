Interpretation of Ratios - Example

A bolt manufacturer can produce 6 bolts per day. He equally distributes all the bolts manufactured for 30 days among his 3 customers.

How much does each customer get?

An assumption is this problem is that all bolts manufactured are equally distributed amongst the three customers. The equal distribution can happen in at least 3 different ways

a) Manufacture all the bolts, and then distribute equally.

b) On a daily basis distribute amongst the three customers the produce for the day

c) Compute the total to be produced, and find the number to be distributed for each customer. Use this number to distribute to first customer, then to second and to third. Ship to one customer at a time.

Solution Method a) : Manufacture all the bolts, and then distribute equally

Number of bolts manufactured per day = 6 bolts/ day

Number of days = 30 days

Total number of bolts manufactured = 6 (bolts per day) *30 (days) = 180 bolts

Number  of customers =3 customers

Number of bolts each customer receives = 180 (bolts)/3 (customers)  = 60 bolts per customer (Answer)

Solution Method b) On a daily basis distribute amongst the three customers the produce for the day 

Number of bolts manufactured in a day = 6 bolts /day

Number of Customers = 3 customers

Number of bolts each customer receives every day = 6/3 = 2 (bolts per day per customer)

Number of days = 30.days

Number of bolts each customer receives in 30 days = 2 (bolts per day/customer) * 30 days = 60 bolts per customer

Solution Method c) Ship to one customer at a time

Number of days = 30 days

Number of customers = 3 customer

Number of day’s worth each customer receives = 30 (days) / 3 (customers) = 10 days worth  / customer.

Number of bolts manufactured per day = 6 bolts day worth

Number of bolts each customer gets = 6(bolts per day) * 10 (days worth /customer) = 60 bolts per customer.
