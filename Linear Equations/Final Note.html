Final Note : Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - II

The examples so far demonstrated that the individual equations can be converted to ratios, and then a proportion containing all the equations can be found to solve.

However, a proportion can be formed directly from the given equations, in the form of a proportion. Then principles of proportions can be applied. This approach is same as Elimination method ( Class 10) or the Row Reduced Echelon form method ( Class 11). Below example demonstrates the same.

Solve

    \(2x+3y=17\)       ---- (A)

    \(x+4y=11\)         -----(B)

Forming the Proportion

\(2x+3y:x+4y::17:11\)

Applying equivalent fraction, equivalent to multiplying second equation by 2

\(2x+3y:2x+8y ::17:22\)

Applying Dividendo

\(2x+3y:5y::17:5\)

Multiplying first by 5 and second by 3

\(10x+15y:15y ::85:15\), Applying Dividendo gives

\(10x:15y ::70:15\), dividing both sides by 10/15,

which gives \(x=7 \space and \space y=1\)
