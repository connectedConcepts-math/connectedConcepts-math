Geometrical Reasoning = Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - I

In this note, we will see with an example

  • why the difference in numerator and denominator if the equations on the right side have to be same
  • why the factors in x in both the equations are proportional. Similarly, why the variables in y, in both equations are proportional.

Preliminaries

In both the examples, the algebraic formulation was of the type 

\((x-a) : (y-a) :: m:n\)  [ a years before ]

\((x-b):(y-b) :: p:q\)  [ b years before ]

The approach  used needed  \((m-n)=(p-q)\). When \((m-n) \neq (p-q)\), then equivalent fractions have to be established on right hand side.

\((x-a) : (y-a) :: m:n\)       - (I)

 is of the form

 \(nx+my=d\),                           - (2)     when expanded.

When  every point on the line in equation (2) is subtracted by ‘a’ , then equation of form (1) is formed. 

Both Equation (1) and Eq (2) are lines that are parallel to each other. The line in Eq(1) passes through origin, where as line through Eq (2) passes through \((0,\frac{d}{m})\). Point  \((a,a)\) lies on Eq (1)

Similarly, 

\((x-b):(y-b) :: p:q\)                          -(3)   is of the form

\(qx+py=f\)                                               - (4)

Eq (3).  passes through Origin, and Point \((b,b)\) lies on Eq3.  Eq 3, is line parallel to Eq 4.

If we can consider \((x,y)\) is the point of intersection of Eq2, Eq4, then \((x-a,y-a)\) will lie on Eq1, and \((x-b,y-b)\) will lie on Eq3. Since we are moving, equal units by a (or by b) to left and down,  \((x,y) \space, (x-a,y-a) \space, (x-b,y-b)\) will lie on a line that has an inclination of 45 degree ( slope 1). 

 

Using these preliminaries, we will see through an example how   \((m-n) = (p-q)\). And through it, find value of \((x,y)\) using an example.

In the picture below 

L1' and L2 ' are two equations, of the ratio form [ Eq1, Eq3]/.

In Triangle, HDB, \(GD=m \space DB = n\). Point B is \((x-a,y-a)\).

IN triangle HFC, \(HF=p \space FC=q\). Point C is \((x-c,y-c)\) .

Since points B and C line on a line with slope 1, EB = EC, showing that  \(p-m=q-n\). This is same as  \(m-n=p-q\).

 

Also, same picture tells us

\((x-a):(x-b) ::m:p\)        and 

\((y-a):(y-b) :: n:q\)

This gives us the proportional relationship.

This approach shifts origin to point of intersection

Another way to look at this approach, is to think about it as if the coordinate system axis are transferred to the lines under consideration, and the origin in original coordinate system is shifted to the point of intersection of the lines.

Akgebraically , if the equations of lines in orthogonal coordinate axis as

\(y=m_{1}x+b_{1}   \space and \space y = m_{2}x+b_{2}\)   with usual notation.

At the point of intersection \((x_{p},y_{p})\), the x and y coordinates on both lines are same.

\(y_{p}=m_{1}x_{p}+b_{1}   \space and \space y_{p} = m_{2}x_{p}+b_{2}\)

Subtracting we get,

\(x_{p} = \frac{b_{2}-b_{1}}{m_{2}-m_{1}}\) and 

\(y_{p} = \frac{m_{1}b_{2}-m_{2}b_{1}}{m_{1}-m_{2}}\)

which is the same scaling that can be observed from the similar triangles [ Left as an exercise]
