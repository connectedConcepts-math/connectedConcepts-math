Example using Unitary method

A grocer sells two kinds of packets N and S. N contains 5kg and 3 Kg Rice. S contains 2 Kg Rice and 1 Kg Wheat. A man needs 26 Kg rice and 15 kg Wheat. How many bags of N and S should he buy. ( Assume that the grocer will take back any number of packets of type N or S - Return policy).

Solution

Let \(x\) packets of N and  \(y\) packets of S be required to buy 26 Kg of Rice and 15 Kg of wheat.

Then,

\(5x+2y=26\) [ Rice ]

\(3x+y=15\) [ Wheat ]

Extended Unitary method would suggest us to find Number of packets required to buy 1 Kg rice ( and 0 Kg wheat ), and Similarly, find Number of packets required to buy 1 Kg Wheat ( and Zero G Rice}

We will Solve for each of them.

Number packets of N and S required for 1 Kg Rice ( 0 Kg Wheat )

\(5x+2y=1\)

\(3x+y=0\)

Solving (using substitution method), gives us \(x=-1 , y=3\). This means

Repackage the balance in terms of N or S. It is clear that the balance can be repackaged into 1 Pkt of N, and this sis returned back to shopkeeper

Number packets of N and S required for 1 Kg What ( 0 Kg Rice )

\(5x+2y=0\)

\(3x+y=1\)

Solving gives \(x=2 , y=-5\)

Package 10Kg Rice and 5 Kg Wheat as 5 Packets of S and return it.

Summary :

Interpretation of table

-x+2y = 1 N

3x-5y = 1 S

(-1) #N per kg of R  x Kg of R + 2(#of N per kg of wheat)  y (kg of wheat) = 1 N

Final Solution

To buy 26 Kg Rice and 15 Kg Wheat

\(26*(-1)  \space of N  \space + 26*(3) \space of S\)    [ 26 times packets required for 1 Kg of Rice]

\(15*(2) \space of N \space + 15*(-5) \space of S\)   [ 15 times packets required for 1 Kg of Wheat]

Adding together, to find total packets of N and S required

\(4 \space of N \space and  \space 3 \space of S\)

Extension of example

Having solved the problem above, and using learnings from it, find number of packets of N and S required to be bought by a person who needs 45 Kg Rice and 26 Kg Wheat.

\(45*(-1)  \space of N  \space + 26(3) \space of S\)   [ 45 times packets required for 1 Kg of Rice]

\(26*(2) \space of N \space + 26*(-5) \space of S\)   [ 15 times packets required for 1 Kg of Wheat]

Adding together,

\(7 \space of N \space and  \space 4 \space of S\)

Latent concepts

The solution to pair of equations  

Pair 1:

\(5x+2y=1\)

\(3x+y=0\)

Pair 2:

\(5x+2y=0\)

\(3x+y=1\)

has captured the inverse of the matrix \(\begin{pmatrix}    5 & 2 \\    3 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) to be  \(\begin{pmatrix}    -1 & 2 \\    3 & -5 \end{pmatrix}\). 

The solution in matrix form \(Ax=b\) is obtained by \(x = A^{-1}b\)
