Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - I

Preliminaries

Internal and External Division of a Number

Dividing a number \(p\)  internally in the ratio \(m:n\), would give two numbers

\(\frac{mp}{(m+n)} , \frac{np}{(m+n)}\)

Diving a number \(p\) externally in the ration \(m:n\) would give two numbers

\(\frac{mp}{(m-n)} , \frac{np}{(m-n)}\)

Converting \(ax+by=c\) into ratios

Using internal division

First  convert \(c\) into sum of two numbers divided internally in the ratio of \(a:b\), which gives 

\(c= \frac{ca}{a+b} + \frac{cb}{a+b}\)

Substituting in \(ax+by=c\) and getting all terms together gives

  \(a(x-\frac{c}{a+b}) + b(y-\frac{c}{a+b}) =0\)

Using External Division

First  convert \(c\) into sum of two numbers divided externally in the ratio of \(a:b\), which gives 

\(c= \frac{ca}{a-b} + \frac{cb}{a-b}\)

Substituting in \(ax+by=c\) and getting all terms together gives

  \(a(x-\frac{c}{a-b}) + b(y+\frac{c}{a-b}) =0\)   if \(a>b\)

\(a(x+\frac{c}{b-a}) + b(y-\frac{c}{b-a}) =0\)   if \(a <b\)

Depending upon the orientation of the equation, either internal division or external division will give the equations of the Form I.

 Examples demonstrate the entire process.
