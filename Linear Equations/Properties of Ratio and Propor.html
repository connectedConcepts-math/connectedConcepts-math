Properties of Ratio and Proportions

Property 1

If four quantities be proportional, the product of the extremes is equal to the product of the means

or if \(a:b :: c:d\) then  \(ad=bc\)

Property 2

If the product of two quantities be equal to the product of two others, the four quantities shall be proportional , i.e., if 

\(ad=bc\). tehn \(\frac a b = \frac c d\)

Property 3 : Invertendo

if \(a:b :: c:d\)  then  \(b:a :: d:c\)

Property 4 :  Componendo

if \(a:b :: c:d\) then  \(a+b:b:: c+d:d\)

Property 5 : Dividendo

if \(a:b :: c:d\) then  \(a-b:b :: c-d:d\)

Property 6 : Convertendo

if \(a:b :: c:d\) then  \(a:a-b::c:c-d\)

Property 7 : Componendo and Dividendo

if \(a:b :: c:d\) then  \(a+b :a-b :: c+d :c-d\)

Property 11

If \(a:b :: c:d\)                 -------- (A) then 

\(la + mb : pa +qb :: lc + md : pc + qd\)

Proof:

Multiplying \(\frac{l}{m}\)on both sides, we get

\(la:mb:lc:nd\), applying componendo on this gives

\(la+mb:mb:: lc+nd:nd\)              -(B)

Similarly, multiplying by \(\frac{p}{q}\) on both sides of (A) gives

\(pa:qb:pc:qd\) , and applying componendo gives

\(pa+qb:qb:: pc+qd:qd\)                 - (C)

Dividing (B) and (C) gives

\(la + mb : pa +qb :: lc + md : pc + qd\)

This property is very useful because, most of the times measurements are not in terms of  a,b,c,d however in the form of \(la+mb\).  This forms the basis for linear equations, and needed to find a,b,c,d.

A typical example is that instead of knowing the unit price of a kg of rice or unit price of kg of wheat, when we buy in super market, we are told that 2 kg rice together with 3 kg of wheat costs Rs 100.

Similarly when measuring concentrations of chemicals, it may not be possible to measure concentration of each compound ( due to measuring difficulties, reactivities etc),, experimenters will measure concentration of mixtures and then estimate the individual concentration.
