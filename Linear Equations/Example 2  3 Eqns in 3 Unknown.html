Example 2: Solution to Equations by converting to Ratios - II

This example explores the extension of technique to three equations in 3 unknowns.

Solve

\(-x+2y+2z=3\)     ----(I)

\(x-y+z=3\)             -----(II)

\(x+y-2z=6\)          -----(III)

Convert to Ratios

\(\frac{x+3}{y+z} =\frac{2}{1}\)        ------------(A)

\(\frac{y+3}{x+z} =\frac{1}{1}\)        -----------(B)

\(\frac{z+3}{x+y} =\frac{1}{2}\)       ----------(C)

Adding 1 on both sides to each of (A),(B),(C) gives

\(\frac{x+y+z+3}{y+z} =\frac{3}{1}\)

\(\frac{x+y+z+3}{x+z} =\frac{2}{1}\)

\(\frac{x+y+z+3}{x+y} =\frac{3}{2}\)

This gives 

\((x+y+z+3):(y+z):(x+z):(x+y)::6:2:3:4\)

Using principle that if

\(p:q:r:s::j:k:l:m\)  then  \(p:(q-p):(r-p):(s-p) :: j:(k-j):(l-j):(m-j)\)

\((x+y+z+3):-(x+3):-(y+3):(-z+3):: 6:-4:-3:-2\)

Adding second, third, and fourth element to the first, we get

\(-6:-(x+3):-(y+3):-(z+3):: -3:-4:-3:-2\)

Applying equivalent fractions gives

\(-6:-(x+3):-(y+3):-(z+3):: -6:-8:-6:-4\),

which gives \(x=5 \space y=3 \space z=1\)

Comments

Ratio with respect to x+y+z was derived after the initial ratio in this problem. However, this can be done with every equation initially itself, as will be seen in the next example.
