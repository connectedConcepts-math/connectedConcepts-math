Connected Concepts

"Everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things."

- Law of Geography

 This law very succinctly brings out the importance of connectedness.   “Spatial analysis” in geography would have been difficult without first law of geography. Though it was written in view of Geography, it captures the essence in Mathematics. While some things may appear far, their nearness is appreciated by knowing the underlying “relatedness”.

Why Connected Concepts?

The Problems in real-life do not present themselves with clear cut data to solve. Real life problems are typically solved by “connecting the dots”.  If the students are aware that the “dot can be connected”, they will explore. This book will help teachers to bring out that awareness.

Need for a book on this topic 

There are many books on school mathematics books including the school textbooks. that are oriented to the curriculum prescribed per Class by their board. Interestingly so, these books have different structure of the content, as the authors view on flow of content. That there are various flows amongst the concepts (but spread in different books) is in itself an existing indication that there are “connected concepts” in different order.

There are many other books that are oriented to provide with lots of examples and exercises .They are organized with the view that students will figure out the relationship amongst the concepts by solving numerous examples.. This approach works only with students who are aware about “connected concepts” and consciously exercise towards identifying them.

The challenge with bringing out the view of “connected concepts” and the importance  of it through these books is ln itself a daunting task for the teachers, despite teachers desire to bring about existence of “connected concepts”.  Some of the reason for these are 

  • Connected concepts (few of them), though directly referenced, are spread across the chapters in various classes. Teachers find it difficult to guide students through multiple textbooks/chapters , while their focus is on particular concept per lesson plan.
  • Connected concepts are hidden within the examples and exercises. While solving these exercises, the usual tendency is to complete the exercise and not focus on the “interpretations” and “implication" so as to obtain a larger view.  Many times the exercises are designed such that with  extra thought, the concepts in the following chapters/classes can be built ( from the learnt concepts). However, again since these relations are spread across multiple chapters/classes and lack of awareness of existence of such links impends learning the “connections”
  • Many connected concepts are difficult to be learnt and recognized, specially when mathematics is treated and taught as different branches - Arithmetic,, Algebra, Geometry  and Calculus.

What will this book cover ?

Will cover the interconnectedness across various topics,. This will teachers to help students

  • To develop or build concepts from what they already know. 
  • To guide students to appreciate the concepts by letting them know how it will be used in future,
  • To build a problem-solving mindset in students..
