Example 3: Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - II

Solve

\(2x+3y+4z=18\)

\(3x+4y+5z=36\)

\(x+2y+3z=42\)

Identify Proportions with x+y+z

Re-writing each equation as below

\(2(x+y+z) +(y+3z)=18\)

\(3(x+y+z) + (y+2z) = 36\)

\((x+y+z) +(y+2z) = 42\)

Since second and third equation have same terms with different ratios,  No solution exists.

Notes

Equations that are linear combinations do not have solutions. This is a concept described in Matrices in Class 11. This can be seen by expressing the equations in ratios.

Insights into Design of Experiment :

This also tells us that while doing experiments, and collecting data, it is important to have variations in data, and not collect data that have proportional relationships in input.  For example, to understand how many moles of a compound A is needed to react with two other compounds B and C. It is not a good idea to conduct the experiment where first case say takes 1 mole each of B and C, and second test takes 2 moles each of B and C. Rather the second experiment could contain 2 moles of B and 0.5 mole of C.
