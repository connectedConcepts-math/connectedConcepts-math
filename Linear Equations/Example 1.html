Example 1 : Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - I

Solve

\(2x+3y = 20\)

\(5x+4y = 43\)

Step 1: Convert to Form 1 using internal division

\(2(x-4) + 3(y-4) =0\)

\(5(x-43/9) + 4(y-43/9) =0\)

Step 2: Convert to ratios

\(\frac{x-4}{y-4} = - \frac{3}{2}\)                   - (I)

\(\frac{(x-\frac{43}{9})} {(y-\frac{43}{9})} = -\frac{4}{5}\)             - (II)

Step 3: Make the difference of numerator and denominator on right side, the same

Difference in numerator and denominator for first equation is  \((-3)-2 = -5\), and of second equation is \((-4)-5 =-9\).

Multiply  (I) by 9, and (II) by 5

\(\frac{x-4}{y-4} = - \frac{27}{18}\) 

\(\frac{(x-\frac{43}{9})} {(y-\frac{43}{9})} = -\frac{20}{25}\)   ( makes difference as -45)

which gives \((x-4): (x-\frac{43}{9}) :: 27:20\)

\((x-4):\frac{7}{9}::27:7\) ( Using Componendo)

Which gives \(z=7\)

Similarly,

\((y-4): (y-\frac{43}{9}) :: 18:25\)

\((y-4):\frac{7}{9}::18:-7\), which gives  \((y-4)=-2 \space or \space y=2\)
