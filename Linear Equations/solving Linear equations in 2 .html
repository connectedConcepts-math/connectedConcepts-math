solving Linear equations in 2 variables using Similar Triangles

We have seen that Linear equations expressed as proportions have ratios within them. We have also seen that the properties of proportions use Similar triangles * Derivation of Basic Proportionality theorem). We take the same age problem and solve using similar triangles here.

Example 1

Ratio of John's age to his Father 10 years ago is 1:5. Six years later, the ratio of their ages becomes 3:7.  Find their current ages.

Some Preliminaries

In the context of the problem, we know the following facts

  • Age increases by 1 year, every year.
  • Age difference between Father and Son, remains the same the end of every year.

Using these facts, we can plot a graph of ages of Father and John, with Y-axis as Age, and X-axis as Year. Since we do not know, when exactly Father and John were born, we can mark any two points on X- axis ( the year they were born) - from these two points, we can draw a 45 degree line (Age increases by 1 year , every year ; meaning slope of the line will be 1).

From the picture above, we can see

Following triangles are similar

SBP and RBJ, MAP and NAJ, XTP and YTJ

Considering Triangles, SBP and RBJ

\(\frac{(d+x)}{x} = \frac{5}{1}\)   which gives  \(d=4x\) ( applying dividendo property of ratios)

Considering Triangles MAP and NAP

\(\frac{(d+16)}{(x+6)} = \frac{7}{3}\)   , Substituting \(d=4x\) (from above) , gives \(d= 32 \space x=8\)

Which tells us that the difference in the age of Father and age of John is 32.

From Triangles XTP and YTP,

\(\frac{(d+x+10)}{(x+10)} = \frac{XT}{YT}\),  Also \(JT = YT\)  and  \(PT=XT\) ( 45 degree )

Solving gives \(XT = 50 \space and  YT =18\)
