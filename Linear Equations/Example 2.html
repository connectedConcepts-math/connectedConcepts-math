Example 2: Solution to Pair of Equations by converting to Ratios - I

Solve

\(2x+3y = 20\)       - (A)

\(4x-5y = 18\)       - (B)

Step 1: Convert to ratio form

Eq (A) and Eq(B) can be converted to Form I by using internal division

\(2(x-4) + 3(y-4) =0\)

\(4(x+18) - 5(y+18) =0\)

Step 2: Convert Into Ratios

\(\frac{(x-4)}{(y-4)} = -\frac{3}{2}\)

\(\frac{(x+18)}{(y+18)} = \frac{5}{4}\)

Step 3: Make the difference of numerator and denominator on right side, the same

Difference of numerator and denominator for Eq (A) is \((-3)-2 = -5\), and for Eq (B) is \(5-4=1\).

LCM of (-5,1) is -5. So, multiply EQ(A) by 1, and Eq (B) by -5.

\(\frac{(x-4)}{(y-4)} = -\frac{3}{2}\)

\(\frac{(x+18)}{(y+18)} = \frac{-25}{-20}\)

This gives

\((x-4):(x+18)::3:25\)

\((x-4):22: 3: 22\) ( Applying Componendo)

\(x=7\)

Similarly, \((y-4):(y+18)::2:-20\)

\((y-4):22: 2: -22\), giving  \(y=2\)
