Geometric Understanding - Vectors

This section continues with the same numerical example, to understand the solution geometrically.

It has been observed from the solution the following

To buy 1 kg of rice means to return 1 packets of N ( or -1 of N)  and 3 packets of S. Similarly,

To Buy 1 Kg of wheat would mean to buy 2  packets of N and return 5 packets of S ( or -5 of S).

Geometric Construction.

Take two orthogonal axes representing N ( number of packets of N) and S ( Number of packets of S).

On this plot, 1 kg of rice is represented by the line segment OA, with point A being (-1,3).

Similarly 1kg of wheat will be represented by segment OB, with point B being (2,-5).

To mark 26 kg of rice in this picture would mean to scale line segment OA, 26 times, which will land at the pint OC, where C being (-26,78). 

Similarly, 15 kg of rice would mean extending the line segmenting OB to OD, such that OD is 15 times OB, leading D to be the point (30,-75).

These two lines OA and OB provided us the direction to add for additional kg of Rice and Wheat respectively.

But the problem asks for 26 kg of rice and 15 kg of wheat,

So from OC, we move in the direction of OB, adding 1kg of wheat at a time ( Draw a line parallel to OB from C, with a length equal to OB). We could also start at D (the point representing required kg of wheat) and go in a parallel direction  for every kg of rice until 26 kg is met. These two lines intersect at point P, which is the required number of N and S, and in this case is (4,3).

 

Another way to interpret

The x-coordinate of C represents the number of N required, and x-coordinate of D represents number of N required for (26,15). Adding these x-ccordinates gives \(-26+30=4\)  of N. Similarly,  adding y-ccordinates of points C and D gives number of packets of S required \(78-75=3\)
